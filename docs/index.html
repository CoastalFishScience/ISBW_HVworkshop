About this workshop:

Welcome to our Hypervolume workshop! The goal of this workshop is to familiarize participants with the concept of hypervolumes, case studies and applications, and how to run the analyses in R. We will start the workshop by providing background and theory on hypervolumes. We will then present multiple case studies using hypervolumes in seagrass and other ecosystems. These case studies will be followed by an interest survey and a walkthrough of the data and R code used to conduct the hypervolume analyses. R scripts and markdown/quarto files will be provided to encourage participants to conduct hypervolume analysis on their own data and promote comparative cross system studies.

Hypervolume modelling is a flexible multivariate modelling technique based on Hutchinson’s n-dimensional hypervolume concept of a niche that describes the ecological requirements of an individual, species, or population in order to persist in a location. Hypervolumes are defined as a set of points that enclose all values of n variables in n-dimensional space. Advances led by Blonder and colleagues (see references) provide multivariate kernel density estimation methods that allows for construction of hypervolumes with a range of variables that expand applications of hypervolumes to a variety of ecological and evolutionary questions and processes beyond niche theory. Examples include quantifying ecosystem status following restoration, mapping the resilience of coastal habitats, and determining the impact of disturbance on trophic functioning (see resources). By leveraging the concept of hypervolumes, seagrass ecologists can gain a more nuanced and comprehensive understanding of their complex interactions and dynamics, facilitatiing conservation, management and restoration.
